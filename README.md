# Superstore Sales and Profit Analysis

> Analysed 9,994 retail orders ($2.3M in sales) to find why profit lagged, and uncovered a top-selling product line that is quietly losing money.

![Dashboard](<Superstore Sales and Profit Overview.png)

## Business Problem
A retail superstore was growing sales steadily, but leadership needed to know whether that growth was actually profitable, and where money might be leaking.

## Objective
Identify the products, regions, and time periods that drive (and drain) profit, and recommend where to act.

## Data
- **Source:** Sample Superstore dataset (Kaggle, public).
- **Scope:** 9,994 orders, 4 years, US market, 17 sub-categories, 4 regions.
- **Confidentiality:** 100% public sample data. No client or proprietary information.

## Tools and Techniques
Tableau: interactive dashboard with KPI cards, a choropleth map, a diverging profit bar chart, a time-series trend, and cross-filtering.

## Key Findings
- Total sales **$2.3M**, total profit **$286K**, overall margin **12.5%**.
- **Tables** is a top seller by revenue but **loses about $17,700** in profit, the single biggest loss-maker.
- **Bookcases** (around -$3,500) and **Supplies** also run at a loss.
- **Furniture** is by far the weakest category ($18K profit versus $145K for Technology).
- The **Central region** underperforms the others on profit.
- Sales dip in February and peak in Q4 (September and November strongest), a clear seasonal pattern.

## Recommendation and Business Impact
- Review pricing and discounting on **Tables and Bookcases**. Heavy discounts are the likely cause of the negative margins. Capping discounts on these lines could recover most of the roughly $21K combined loss.
- Treat **Furniture** as a margin-repair priority, not a growth priority.
- Investigate **Central region** operations for cost or discounting issues.
- Plan inventory and staffing to ramp **before the Q4 peak**.
